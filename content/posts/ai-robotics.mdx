## VLA Models: The Robot Brain Revolution

Here's the technical core of why everything is accelerating: **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models**.

The old way of building robot intelligence was a pipeline: perception module → planning module → control module. Information got lost between stages. Real-time adaptation was nearly impossible.

VLA models change everything. Camera pixels go in, joint motor commands come out. One neural network. End to end. Pixels to actions.

Google DeepMind's Karol Hausman nailed it:

> *"Looking at the history of robotics, we've always been bottlenecked by intelligence."*

VLA models are uncorking that bottleneck. Figure's Helix 02 uses a three-layer system — System 0 for balance, System 1 for actions, System 2 for reasoning — that mirrors how neuroscientists think about human motor control. It can open pill bottles, manipulate syringes, and navigate rooms, all through a single model.

The big question is data. ChatGPT ate the internet's text. What does a robot eat? Simulations, teleoperation recordings, YouTube cooking videos? Figure AI's "Project Go-Big" aims for internet-scale robot pretraining. Physical Intelligence open-sourced π₀. The moment the data bottleneck breaks could be robotics' true ChatGPT moment.

## The Deployment Tiers: When Robots Take Which Jobs

The research paints a clear timeline:

**Tier 1 (2025-2026):** Structured, simple tasks. Logistics sorting, basic assembly assist, repetitive inspection. *This is happening now.*

**Tier 2 (2027-2028):** Semi-structured complex tasks. Variable manufacturing, precision assembly. *Companies are preparing for this.*

**Tier 3 (2029-2030+):** Unstructured environments. Home assistance, elderly care, disaster response. *This is the consumer revolution.*
