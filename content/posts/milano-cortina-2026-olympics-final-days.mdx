## The Story Everyone Is Talking About: Alysa Liu

If you only follow one athlete from these Olympics, make it **Alysa Liu**.

On Thursday, February 19th, the 19-year-old Chinese-American figure skater pulled off one of the great reversals in Olympic history. She entered the women's free skate in **third place** after the short program ‚Äî a deficit that most skaters never overcome at this level. By the time the music stopped, she had climbed to **first**.

It was Team USA's first Olympic gold in women's individual figure skating in **24 years**. The last American woman to claim that title? Sarah Hughes in 2002 at Salt Lake City.

Japan's Kaori Sakamoto took silver, and Ami Nakai earned bronze ‚Äî continuing Japan's remarkable run in figure skating. For Korea, who had been hoping for a breakthrough in the discipline, the podium remained elusive once again, though the performances of Korean skaters drew genuine praise from commentators.

What made Liu's win more than just a score was the narrative: she's a reigning world champion who entered these Olympics facing questions about her ability to perform under Olympic pressure. She answered them loudly.

## USA Women's Hockey: Canada Falls Again

If figure skating was the artistic highlight of Day 13, **women's ice hockey** provided the drama.

Team USA defeated Canada in **overtime** to claim the gold medal ‚Äî a result that's becoming almost routine between these two programs, yet never actually feels routine when you're watching it. The rivalry between American and Canadian women's hockey is arguably the fiercest in winter sports, and it delivered again on the biggest stage.

For the Canadian players, it's another close call. For the Americans, it's further confirmation that this team may be the deepest and most complete they've had in years.

## What Makes These Games Different

Milano Cortina 2026 will be remembered for a few things beyond the results:

**The venues.** Splitting events between Milan, Cortina d'Ampezzo, and other Italian locations created a genuinely beautiful Olympic canvas. Footage of alpine skiing and biathlon against the Dolomites backdrop has been some of the most striking sports television in years.

**The drama-to-events ratio.** This might sound like hollow praise, but the sheer number of competitive events ‚Äî events that weren't decided before the final run, final jump, or final minute ‚Äî has been unusually high. Fewer blowouts, more photo finishes.

**The emergence of new programs.** South Korea in snowboarding. Italy's breakout. Nations performing at levels their histories didn't predict.
