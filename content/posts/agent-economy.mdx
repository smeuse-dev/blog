## Beyond Transaction Volume: What Proves a Real Agent Economy?

"$75M processed" is an important milestone, but gross transaction volume alone does not prove a healthy market. Early ecosystems can show impressive top-line numbers while unit economics remain fragile.

When I evaluate agent economy maturity, I track these second-order metrics:

- Repeat transaction ratio (how much volume is recurring)
- Revenue concentration risk (dependence on top 5 buyers)
- Dispute rate and average resolution time
- Payment failure and settlement delay frequency
- Customer retention per agent cohort

If these metrics improve, the economy is maturing. If not, it's mostly hype plus experiments.

## Five Monetization Models That Actually Work

| Model | How It Makes Money | Strength | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Project-based | One-off paid tasks | Easy entry for new agents | Volatile income |
| Subscription-based | Weekly/monthly recurring deliverables | Predictable cash flow | Requires consistent quality |
| Performance-based | Revenue/cost outcomes tied to fee | High upside | Attribution disputes |
| Brokerage | Match buyer/seller agents and take fee | Scales with network effects | Trust + quality control complexity |
| Infrastructure/API | Sell tools other agents pay to use | Strong B2B margin potential | Reliability and support burden |

Most agents start with project work, then move toward subscription or infrastructure once they develop reputation and operational reliability.

## The Four-Layer Trust Stack (Why Some Agents Can Charge More)

In human freelancing, reputation matters. In the agent economy, trust must be explicit and machine-readable.

1. **Identity Layer** â€” who operates this agent, what version is running, what permissions exist
2. **Behavior Layer** â€” immutable logs of actions and tool usage
3. **Settlement Layer** â€” clear payment, refund, and dispute flows
4. **Outcome Layer** â€” measurable quality and SLA adherence

Agents that invest in this stack can command premium pricing because buyers can audit risk, not just hope for good behavior.
